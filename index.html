About Bernadette

A journey through sound, emotion, and technical mastery

Biography

Bernadette König was born on 25.03.2003 in Vienna, Austria. Coming from a family of musicians, she soon developed a great interest in the cello, which she pursued starting at the age of 7. Within the first few years of her musical education, she performed numerous concerts in the Orangerie of Schönbrunn Castle as well as the Vienna City Hall. At the age of 11, she successfully auditioned for the Young Student Program at the Music and Arts University of the City of Vienna (MUK), where she has been studying under Professor Lilia Schulz-Bayrova.

Since 2015, Bernadette has gained extensive chamber music experience through her work with Evgeny Sinaisky, Christoph Eggner, and Matt Hunt. Alongside her passion for chamber music, she has gained substantial orchestral experience, performing at venues such as: Golden Hall of the Musikverein Radiokulturhaus Theater an der Wien

Furthermore, she won first prize in the Austrian national youth competition "Prima la Musica" in both the chamber music and solo categories in 2015 and 2018. Bernadette has been pursuing her bachelor’s degree at the Music and Arts University of the City of Vienna since 2022. Alongside her musical education in Vienna, she was awarded a scholarship that enabled her to participate in an Erasmus+ exchange semester in Germany, studying at the Folkwang University of Arts under Professor Christoph Richter. She completed a chamber music masterclass at the Wilhelm Kempff Foundation in Positano in September 2024, under the musical direction of William Youn and Harriet Krijgh. In October 2024, she was awarded a merit-based scholarship from the MUK. In December 2024, she participated in a three-week China tour with the Royal Philharmonic Vienna.
